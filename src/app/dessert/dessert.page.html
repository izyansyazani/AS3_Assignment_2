Dessert Recipes
Rice Pudding (Kheer)
Description: Creamy and aromatic rice pudding infused with cardamom and garnished with almonds and raisins. Calories: 350 kcal 1/2 cup basmati rice 4 cups whole milk 1/2 cup sugar 1/4 tsp cardamom powder 2 tbsp sliced almonds 2 tbsp raisins Instructions: 1. Rinse the rice under cold water and drain. 2. In a large saucepan, combine rice and milk and bring to a boil. 3. Reduce heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the mixture is thickened (about 30-40 minutes). 4. Add sugar and cardamom powder and cook for another 5 minutes. 5. Remove from heat and let it cool. Garnish with almonds and raisins before serving.
Coconut Rice Balls (Klepon)
Description: Chewy rice balls filled with palm sugar and coated with grated coconut. Calories: 300 kcal 1 cup glutinous rice flour 1/4 cup palm sugar, chopped 1/2 cup grated coconut Green food coloring (optional) Instructions: 1. In a bowl, mix glutinous rice flour with a little water to form a dough. 2. Divide the dough into small balls and flatten them. 3. Place a piece of palm sugar in the center of each flattened dough and seal the edges to form balls. 4. Boil the balls in water until they float to the surface (about 3-5 minutes). 5. Roll the boiled balls in grated coconut. Add green food coloring to the coconut for a vibrant look if desired.
Mango Lassi
Description: A refreshing yogurt-based drink blended with ripe mangoes and flavored with cardamom. Calories: 320 kcal 1 ripe mango, peeled and chopped 1 cup plain yogurt 1/2 cup milk 2 tbsp sugar (adjust according to sweetness of mango) 1/4 tsp cardamom powder Ice cubes (optional) Instructions: 1. In a blender, combine chopped mango, yogurt, milk, sugar, and cardamom powder. 2. Blend until smooth and creamy. 3. Add ice cubes if using and blend again until frothy. 4. Pour into glasses and serve chilled.
Chocolate Covered Dates
Description: Medjool dates stuffed with almonds and dipped in melted chocolate. Calories: 400 kcal 12 Medjool dates, pitted 12 whole almonds 100g dark chocolate, melted Instructions: 1. Insert one almond into each date where the pit was removed. 2. Dip each stuffed date into melted chocolate to coat. 3. Place the chocolate-covered dates on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate until set. 4. Once set, serve and enjoy!
Semolina Cake (Basbousa)
Description: A sweet and moist cake made from semolina, coconut, and flavored with rosewater. Calories: 450 kcal 1 cup semolina 1/2 cup desiccated coconut 1/2 cup sugar 1/2 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup melted butter 1/4 cup milk 1 tsp baking powder 1/4 cup slivered almonds or pistachios for garnish 1 tbsp rosewater Instructions: 1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a baking dish. 2. In a bowl, mix together semolina, desiccated coconut, sugar, yogurt, melted butter, milk, baking powder, and rosewater to form a batter. 3. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top. 4. Sprinkle slivered almonds or pistachios on top. 5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown and a skewer inserted comes out clean. 6. Allow to cool slightly before cutting into squares or diamonds.
Turkish Delight (Lokum)
Description: Gooey and fragrant Turkish delight made with sugar, cornstarch, and flavored with rosewater or lemon. Calories: 280 kcal 2 cups granulated sugar 1 cup water 1 cup cornstarch 1 tbsp lemon juice 2 tbsp rosewater or orange blossom water Powdered sugar for dusting Instructions: 1. Grease a square baking dish and line it with parchment paper. 2. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil and simmer until the sugar is completely dissolved. 3. In a separate bowl, mix cornstarch with 1 cup of cold water to form a smooth paste. 4. Gradually add the cornstarch mixture to the sugar syrup while stirring continuously. 5. Cook the mixture over low heat, stirring constantly until it thickens and becomes translucent (about 10-15 minutes). 6. Add lemon juice and rosewater or orange blossom water. Mix well. 7. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread evenly. 8. Allow to cool and set for at least 4 hours or overnight. 9. Once set, dust with powdered sugar and cut into squares or rectangles.
Pistachio Baklava Rolls
Description: Crispy layers of phyllo pastry filled with sweet pistachio filling and soaked in sugar syrup. Calories: 420 kcal 1/2 lb phyllo pastry sheets 1 cup unsalted pistachios, finely chopped 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted 1/2 cup sugar 1/4 cup water 1 tbsp lemon juice 1/4 tsp rosewater Instructions: 1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish. 2. In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil and simmer until the sugar is dissolved. Remove from heat and add rosewater. Set aside to cool. 3. Lay out one sheet of phyllo pastry and brush it with melted butter. Place another sheet on top and repeat the process until you have 5-6 layers. 4. Sprinkle a layer of chopped pistachios over the buttered pastry. 5. Roll the pastry tightly to form a log. Repeat the process with the remaining pastry and pistachios. 6. Cut each roll into 2-inch pieces and place them in the greased baking dish. 7. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown and crispy. 8. Pour the cooled sugar syrup over the hot baklava rolls and allow them to soak for at least 4 hours or overnight.
Mango Sago
Description: A refreshing and creamy dessert made from mango puree, sago pearls, and coconut milk. Calories: 380 kcal 1 cup small sago pearls 2 cups water 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced 1 cup coconut milk 1/2 cup sugar Instructions: 1. Rinse the sago pearls under cold water and drain. 2. In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the sago pearls and cook until translucent (about 10-15 minutes). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. 3. Drain the cooked sago pearls and rinse under cold water. Set aside. 4. In a blender, combine the diced mangoes, coconut milk, and sugar. Blend until smooth and creamy. 5. In a serving bowl, layer the cooked sago pearls and mango puree. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used up. 6. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving.
Coconut Macaroons
Description: Chewy and sweet coconut cookies made from shredded coconut, egg whites, and sugar. Calories: 350 kcal 3 cups shredded coconut 3/4 cup sugar 2 egg whites 1/2 tsp vanilla extract 1/4 tsp salt Instructions: 1. Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. 2. In a mixing bowl, combine shredded coconut, sugar, egg whites, vanilla extract, and salt. Mix until well combined. 3. Drop tablespoonfuls of the coconut mixture onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them apart. 4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the macaroons are golden brown around the edges. 5. Allow the macaroons to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
Date and Nut Bars
Description: Nutty and sweet bars made from dates, nuts, and honey. Calories: 320 kcal 1 cup pitted dates 1 cup mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts, pecans) 1/4 cup honey 1/2 tsp vanilla extract 1/4 tsp salt Instructions: 1. In a food processor, combine pitted dates, mixed nuts, honey, vanilla extract, and salt. Process until the mixture comes together and forms a sticky dough. 2. Press the mixture evenly into a lined baking dish. 3. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour to set. 4. Once set, cut into bars or squares. 5. Store the bars in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
