Italian Recipes
Cacio e Pepe
Description: Crispy breaded chicken served with a rich Japanese curry sauce. Calories: 450 kcal 2 boneless chicken breasts 1 cup panko breadcrumbs 1 egg, beaten 2 cups cooked white rice 1 onion, sliced 2 carrots, chopped 2 potatoes, diced 3 cups chicken broth 2 tbsp vegetable oil Instructions: 1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Dip each chicken breast in the beaten egg, then coat with panko breadcrumbs. 2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Fry the chicken until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes per side. Remove and set aside. 3. In the same pan, add more oil if needed and sauté the onion, carrots, and potatoes until softened. 4. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until vegetables are tender. 5. Slice the fried chicken and serve with the vegetable curry over cooked white rice.
Pesto Genovese
Description: Tender chicken marinated in yogurt and spices, grilled and served in a creamy tomato sauce. Calories: 480 kcal 2 boneless chicken breasts, cut into cubes 1 cup plain yogurt 3 tbsp tikka masala spice mix 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes 1 cup heavy cream 2 tbsp vegetable oil Instructions: 1. In a bowl, combine yogurt and tikka masala spice mix. Add chicken cubes and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight in the refrigerator. 2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent. 3. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook until browned on all sides. 4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and simmer for 15-20 minutes. 5. Stir in the heavy cream and cook for an additional 5 minutes until heated through. 6. Serve hot with naan bread or rice.
Caponata
Description: Thinly sliced marinated beef, wrapped in pita bread with fresh veggies and tahini sauce. Calories: 520 kcal 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced 4 pita breads 1 cup plain yogurt 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 lemon, juiced 1 tsp cumin 1 tsp paprika 1 cucumber, thinly sliced 1 tomato, diced 1/2 cup tahini sauce Instructions: 1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, minced garlic, lemon juice, cumin, and paprika. Add beef slices and marinate for at least 1 hour. 2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the marinated beef slices until browned and cooked through. 3. Warm the pita breads in the oven or on a skillet until slightly toasted. 4. Spread a layer of tahini sauce on each pita bread. Add cooked beef slices, cucumber slices, and diced tomatoes. 5. Roll up the pita breads and serve immediately.
Minestrone
Description: A hearty Italian vegetable soup made with fresh vegetables, beans, and pasta. Calories: 210 kcal 1 can (14 oz) cannellini beans, drained 1 cup chopped carrots 1 cup chopped celery 1 cup chopped zucchini 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup small pasta (like ditalini or elbow) 2 tbsp olive oil Instructions: 1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions, carrots, celery, and garlic. Sauté until vegetables are softened. 2. Add zucchini, diced tomatoes, cannellini beans, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes. 3. Add the pasta and cook for an additional 10 minutes or until pasta is tender. 4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with crusty bread.
Osso Buco
Description: Tender veal shanks braised with vegetables, white wine, and broth, served with gremolata. Calories: 580 kcal 4 veal shanks 1 onion, chopped 1 carrot, chopped 1 celery stalk, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes 2 cups beef broth 1 cup dry white wine 2 tbsp olive oil 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 2 tbsp grated lemon zest Instructions: 1. Season veal shanks with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown veal shanks on all sides. Remove and set aside. 2. In the same skillet, add more olive oil if needed. Sauté onion, carrot, celery, and garlic until softened. 3. Add diced tomatoes, beef broth, and white wine. Bring to a boil. 4. Return the browned veal shanks to the skillet. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours or until veal is tender. Check occasionally and add more broth if needed. 5. In a small bowl, combine chopped parsley and grated lemon zest. Sprinkle over the osso buco before serving.
Risotto alla Milanese
Description: Creamy Italian rice dish flavored with saffron, butter, and Parmesan cheese. Calories: 420 kcal 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice 4 cups chicken broth 1 onion, finely chopped 1/2 cup dry white wine 1/2 tsp saffron threads 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 2 tbsp butter 2 tbsp olive oil Instructions: 1. In a small saucepan, heat chicken broth over low heat. Keep warm. 2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and butter over medium heat. Add chopped onion and sauté until translucent. 3. Add Arborio rice to the skillet and stir to coat with the oil and butter. 4. Add white wine to the skillet and stir until absorbed by the rice. 5. Add a ladleful of warm chicken broth to the skillet and stir until absorbed by the rice. Continue adding broth, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly, until rice is tender and creamy. 6. Stir in saffron threads and grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste. 7. Remove from heat, cover, and let rest for 2-3 minutes before serving.
Amatriciana
Description: Spaghetti pasta served with a sauce made from tomatoes, pancetta, and Pecorino cheese. Calories: 480 kcal 8 oz spaghetti pasta 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes 4 oz pancetta, diced 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 cup grated Pecorino cheese 2 tbsp olive oil 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes (optional) Instructions: 1. Cook spaghetti pasta in boiling salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside. 2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced pancetta and sauté until crispy. 3. Add chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet. Sauté until translucent. 4. Add crushed tomatoes and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet. Simmer for 10-15 minutes. 5. Add cooked spaghetti pasta to the skillet and toss to coat with the sauce. 6. Remove from heat and stir in grated Pecorino cheese. Serve hot.
Bruschetta
Description: Toasted bread topped with a mixture of tomatoes, basil, garlic, and olive oil. Calories: 160 kcal 4 slices Italian bread 2 tomatoes, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped 2 tbsp olive oil 1 tsp balsamic vinegar Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: 1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Place bread slices on a baking sheet and toast in the oven until golden brown. 2. In a bowl, combine diced tomatoes, minced garlic, chopped basil, olive oil, and balsamic vinegar. Season with salt and pepper. 3. Top toasted bread slices with the tomato mixture. Serve immediately.
Cannoli
Description: Crispy fried pastry shells filled with a sweet and creamy ricotta cheese mixture. Calories: 280 kcal 8 cannoli shells 1 cup ricotta cheese 1/2 cup powdered sugar 1/4 cup chopped chocolate 1/4 cup chopped pistachios 1/4 tsp vanilla extract 1/4 tsp cinnamon Oil for frying Instructions: 1. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and cinnamon. Mix well. 2. Fold in chopped chocolate and chopped pistachios into the ricotta mixture. 3. Spoon the ricotta mixture into a piping bag fitted with a large star tip. 4. Pipe the ricotta mixture into the cannoli shells, filling from both ends. 5. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large skillet to 375°F (190°C). Fry the filled cannoli shells until golden brown. 6. Drain on paper towels and let cool completely before serving. Dust with powdered sugar if desired.
Tiramisu
Description: Layered dessert made with ladyfingers soaked in coffee and rum, layered with a mascarpone cheese mixture. Calories: 320 kcal 1 cup mascarpone cheese 24 ladyfingers 1 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled 1/2 cup sugar 3 eggs, separated 2 tbsp rum Cocoa powder for dusting Instructions: 1. In a bowl, beat egg yolks and sugar until light and fluffy. 2. Add mascarpone cheese to the egg yolk mixture and beat until smooth. 3. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form. 4. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the mascarpone mixture. Set aside. 5. In a shallow dish, combine cooled coffee and rum. 6. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture and line the bottom of a serving dish. 7. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture. 8. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. 9. Before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder.
